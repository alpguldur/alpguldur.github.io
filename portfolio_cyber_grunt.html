Cyber Grunt is an endless top-down survival shooter developed in Unreal Engine 4 where the objective is to survive as long as possible. Player controls a robot named XRH-44 equipped with a laser gun. Game world contains several tools for the player to utilize and to hold off incoming enemies. Enemies are spawned into the map through a wave system. The number of enemies increases with each subsequent wave.

  • Create a challenging level in UE4 with a fast-paced survival gameplay.
  • Design and balance game mechanics to make the combat encounter engaging.
  • Build an interesting game world by limited amount of UE4 marketplace assets.
  • Design an in-game economy to add variety to the gameplay.

Team Size:  4

Role:  Game Designer

Platform:  Windows PC

Game Engine: Unreal Engine 4

Software Used: Maya,
                           Photoshop

Programming: Unreal Blueprint
Visual Scripting, C++

Duration: 12 weeks

Player Input:

  • Twin stick shooter controls on a gamepad (e.g., Xbox One controller) were used as a foundation to build on.
  • Left thumbstick to move the character.
  • Right thumbstick to aim and shoot at the same time.
  • Right bumper to roll quickly on the ground to escape from a mob of enemies
  • Face buttons to interact with turrets.
  • The gamepad was chosen for the player input because animations for turning 360 degrees around and shooting were much smoother and satisfying than mouse and keyboard.

Enemies:

  • There are three enemies that have unique characteristics.
  • All enemies explode and cause damage to the player once they get in close proximity.

1. Speedy

  • The smallest and the fastest character.
  • Causes the smallest amount of damage.
  • Has the least health.
  • Designed to be more difficult to spot compared to other enemies and sneak up on the player.

2. Grunt

  • Character size and speed are similar to the player.
  • Causes mediocre damage and has medium health.
  • Designed to impose a moderate threat to the player.

3. Heavy

  • The biggest and the slowest character.
  • Causes the highest amount of damage.
  • Has the most health
  • Designed to be easily spotted and escapable from, but also to be most destructive in a tight area.

Variety in enemy characteristics was designed to make the player think which enemy is their primary target in different circumstances to survive the longest.

Enemy AI:

  • All three enemies are scripted to chase the player at all times around the level.
  • Enemies do not rely on sight to spot the player, and they always know where the player is located.
  • Enemies' only target is the player. They do not attack the tools which player can use.
  • Constant pressure enemies put on the player is designed to have challenge aesthetic while maintaining fast-paced action.

Enemy Waves:

  • Unlimited number of waves with an increasing number of enemies in subsequent waves.
  • Enemies are spawned around the level at random locations each wave.
  • The number of each type of enemies spawned at the start of every wave varies.

Point System:

Player earns points for each enemy killed.

  • Speedy: 20 points
  • Grunt: 30 points
  • Heavy: 40 points

These points can be used to activate and upgrade turrets scattered around the level.

Tools:

1. Turrets

  • Friendly turrets that can be activated and upgraded with points the player earns.
  • Turrets shoot the closest enemy in turret's field of view.
  • Designed to assist the player to survive longer especially in later rounds.

2. Jump Pads

  • When stepped on, they quickly move the player from one location to another.
  • Designed to assist the player to escape from a large number of enemy groups.
  • Located at several parts of the level.
